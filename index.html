A Letter From Re Perez

In November 2025, I was diagnosed with prostate cancer.

I know what happens when you say the word cancer.

People project their fear onto you, fill in the story before you've told it, and suddenly you're defined by something you never asked to be defined by.

Right now, I am fine. It's low risk.
The prognosis is favorable.
Even a "good" diagnosis forces you to re-evaluate everything you're doing in your life.

Shortly after the diagnosis, a brother of mine called me immediately and asked, "How are you?"

I was walking outside in Austin. I stopped in my tracks. I burst into tears.

Because for the first time in a very long time, despite having a great network of friends, I felt genuinely alone. That was the moment I understood my own loneliness.

A health scare doesn't care about your accomplishments, your revenue, your roster, or your reputation. It strips all of that away and asks the only questions that count.

Do you love your life? And if so, can you love higher?

I sat with those questions.

Yes, I do love my life. And I am ready to love higher.
Within myself. In my relationships. In business.

And I want to do that with a brotherhood of conscious gay men who are asking the same questions.

For the past 20 years, I've sat in some of the best business masterminds and entrepreneur groups in the world. And yet I was usually the only gay man in the room. Not because I was hiding.

Because there was no room built for me.

I have looked in the gay rooms too:

The LGBTQ+ professional networks.
The gay business associations.
The social scenes.

You know what I could not find?

Gay men who have done both the inner work and the outer work. Gay men who show up as equals, not competitors. Gay men who come together as peers for intimacy and deep connection, not for the party scene or hooking up.

I looked for that room. It did not exist. So I decided to build it.
