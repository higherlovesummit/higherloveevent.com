The Room You Didn't Know Existed. Until Now.

Hi, I'm Re Perez.

As the founder of Higher Love, I feel it's important to share how this all came to be.

In November 2025, I was diagnosed with prostate cancer. I know what happens when you say the word cancer. People project their fear onto you, fill in the story before you've told it, and suddenly you're defined by something you never asked to be defined by. Rest assured, I am fine. Even a "good" diagnosis forces you to re-evaluate everything you're doing in your life.

Shortly after the diagnosis, a brother of mine called me immediately and asked, "How are you?"

I was walking outside in Austin. I stopped in my tracks. I burst into tears. Because for the first time in a very long time, despite having a great network of friends, I felt genuinely alone. That was the moment I understood my own loneliness.

A health scare doesn't care about your accomplishments, your revenue, your roster, or your reputation. It strips all of that away and asks the questions that count.

Did you love fully... or did you love carefully?

I sat with those questions.

After reflecting on my career, I've been blessed to participate in some of the most elite business masterminds and entrepreneur groups. The only thing is that I was usually the only gay man in the room.

I've explored all gay or LGBTQ+ organizations. They were fine but they didn't seem to fit my vibe.

You know what I could not find?

Gay men who gather in brotherhood, as peers, not competitors. Gay men who have done both the inner and outer work. Gay men who seek deep, authentic connection over the usual party scenes.

I looked for that room. It did not exist. So I decided to build it.

Three Worlds. One Room.

I asked myself: what are the top three worlds that have influenced my life... and my business?

The first is entrepreneurship. Business. Growth. Systems. AI. The cutting edge of what is changing in the world right now. I find this through the business masterminds and I am still very much in those rooms. There is real value there. I learn constantly. I stay sharp.

The second is wellness and longevity. As I have gotten older, this has become non-negotiable. Mindfulness. Meditation. Bodywork. Somatic work. The food I eat. The way I move. Working out and staying fit, not for vanity, but for the kind of energy a full, vibrant life requires.

The third is personal growth and development. For decades, I have immersed myself in understanding who we are and why we think, behave, and feel the way we do. The disciplines that keep the mind sharp and the spirit awake.

My thesis for Higher Love is that something magical can be created for conscious gay men if I were to create a room with three worlds combined.

Picture This With Me.

A gorgeous, beautiful estate just outside Austin. A private chef who understands what we are doing here. Saunas. Cold plunges. Stunning views. Breathwork, bodywork, meditation, led by world-class facilitators. Three full days of being met, challenged, and restored.

Now picture all of that built exclusively for conscious gay men who are accomplished, doing the inner work, and ready to go deeper.

Why In-Person?

The most profound relationships in my life were built in three days, dropped in deep with someone, face to face, no screens.

In the AI era, nothing beats in-person connection. If you are looking for another online community, or you are not willing to travel for the right room, this isn't the room for you.

The gay men you meet in September could very well be the brothers you have been looking for. I hope you are one of them.

If you know this is your room, let's have a conversation.

Frequently Asked Questions

This is not a conference. There are no panels, no keynotes, no lanyards.

The Higher Love Summit is a fully immersive, three-day experience designed around the specific men in the room. The programming evolves to meet who you actually are — not who you present at a business dinner.

Expect a mix of deep inner work, physical adventure, and sensory experiences that will move you in ways you didn't anticipate. Days flow from mind to body to integration. Some moments will challenge you. Others will restore you completely.

There are surprises built into every day. That is by design. Part of what makes this work is the willingness to surrender to what's been curated for you.

I have spent over two decades in the relationship business. My reputation is built on one thing: creating experiences where people feel genuinely seen, genuinely nourished, and genuinely changed. You can look that up. You can ask the men who referred you. Either way, you'll find the same answer.

Every seat at this Summit was chosen. Not filled. Chosen.

The selection process begins with a conversation. I'm looking for men who have built something real and are honest about the cost of that. Men who have an active inner life, not just a wellness habit they mention on LinkedIn. Men who are done being the most accomplished person in every room and are ready for peers, not an audience.

I ask specific questions in that conversation. What you're building and what it means to you beyond the business. What your personal growth practice looks like today. What genuine brotherhood would actually give you that you don't already have.

I'm not looking for the right answers. I'm looking for the right depth.

This is an invitation-only gathering. The room stays small by design. A seat that isn't the right fit won't be filled — regardless of what someone is willing to pay.

Everything except your airfare.

Accommodations, all meals, bodywork, wellness treatments, curated adventures, and the full three-day experience are covered. You arrive. You receive. You leave changed.

The founding investment is $7,500. That number reflects what it actually costs to create an experience at this level — nothing more. It is a commitment to building the room right, which means sparing nothing on the quality of what happens inside it.

What you cannot put a number on is the room itself. One honest conversation with the right man at the right moment has changed the trajectory of careers, companies, and lives. That is what a room full of 12 carefully selected men makes possible. But the men who have said yes to rooms like this don't talk about the cost. They talk about what shifted.
